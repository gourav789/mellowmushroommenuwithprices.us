Mellow Mushroom FAQ

Complete answers to the most common questions about the Mellow Mushroom menu, prices, dietary options, locations, and how to order.

Pricing Questions

A large (16") specialty pizza at Mellow Mushroom typically runs $24–32 depending on the location and specific toppings. A basic cheese or pepperoni large is usually $20–24, while the more complex specialty pies with premium toppings land in the $26–32 range. Prices vary by location. A downtown restaurant in a high-rent market will typically charge $2–5 more than a suburban location for the same pie.
No. Menu prices do not include sales tax. Expect to add 6–10% depending on your city and state. Delivery fees, service fees from third-party apps, and tips are also additional. For dine-in with tip and tax on a $50 food order, budget $60–65 total.
Most Mellow Mushroom restaurants are independently owned franchises. Individual owners set their own menu prices based on local rent, labor, and ingredient costs. A large pizza in a lower-cost suburban area might run $4–6 less than in a high-rent downtown or tourist area location. Always check the online menu for your specific location's address to get current accurate pricing.
A family of four spending on food only can expect $60–90 before tax and tip. Practical breakdown: one large specialty pizza ($26–30) + one appetizer ($9–13) + two kids meals ($12–18) + beverages ($10–14) = $57–75 before tax. Add 8% tax and 18% tip and you are looking at $72–95 total for a full dine-in experience.
The base menu prices are usually the same. What changes is the total because delivery adds fees. Third-party delivery apps (DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub) typically add a delivery fee ($3–5), service fee ($3–4), and delivery driver tip ($5–8) on top of menu prices — adding $11–17+ to your total. Pickup avoids all of these costs.
Yes — several practical strategies: (1) Choose pickup over delivery to eliminate $10–17+ in fees and tips. (2) Order a gallon of sweet tea or lemonade ($12–14) instead of individual drinks for a table of 4–6. (3) Share large pizzas and appetizers — larger sizes are cheaper per slice. (4) Ask about weekday specials — some locations run offers not listed on apps. (5) Skip extra premium toppings on first visits to taste the base version before adding extras.

Menu Questions

The House Special is consistently the most ordered pizza at most locations — loaded with pepperoni, Italian sausage, mushrooms, green peppers, onions, and double mozzarella. Other perennial favorites include the Super Pepp, Mighty Meaty, Holy Shiitake, Great White, and Funky Q Chicken. The Build Your Own option is also extremely popular for customers with specific preferences.
Three sizes: Small (10"), Medium (14"), and Large (16"). A small serves 1–2 people, a medium serves 2–3, and a large serves 4–5 people. Gluten-free crust is typically only available in a smaller personal size at most locations.
The Pretzel Bites with beer cheese dip are consistently the most recommended starter — they arrive quickly, share easily, and pair perfectly with beer. The 8 Mellow Rollz are also highly recommended for larger groups. The Spinach Artichoke Dip is popular with those looking for a more substantial vegetarian starter. For something different, the Greek Feta Rollz are worth trying.
Sauce options vary by location but typically include: Mild Buffalo, Medium Buffalo, Hot Buffalo, Extra Hot, Lemon Pepper, Honey BBQ, Honey Sriracha, Garlic Parmesan, Nashville Hot, and dry rub options. Each wing order comes with one dipping sauce choice (ranch or blue cheese). Ask your server about current sauce availability at your specific location.
Yes. The Build Your Own Pizza option gives you full control over crust size, sauce type, cheese selection, and toppings. You can also modify existing signature pies by asking for changes. Basic modifications (removing toppings, swapping standard sauces) typically cost nothing extra. Adding premium ingredients or making major substitutions may add $1–4 per change.
Yes. The kids menu includes Mac & Cheese and French Bread Pizza (available in cheese or pepperoni). Kids meals typically run $6–10 and often include a drink. The format works well for younger diners who want their own meal while adults share larger pizzas at the table.

Dietary Questions

Yes. Certified gluten-free crust is available at most locations, and several specialty pizzas have GF versions (House Special, Mighty Meaty, Veg Out, Kosmic Karma, and BYO). A gluten-free brownie is available for dessert. Important: cross-contamination with gluten is possible in any busy kitchen. Guests with celiac disease should speak directly with the kitchen before ordering.
Yes, though availability varies by location. Vegan cheese is available at many locations. Key vegan options include: Vegan Cheese Pizza, Vegan Veg-Out, Vegan Build Your Own Pizza, Tempeh Hoagie, Vegan Avocado Hoagie, Vegan Apple Harvest Salad, and Vegan Keep It Greek Salad. Call your specific location ahead of time to confirm vegan cheese and ingredient availability before visiting specifically for vegan options.
Vegetarians have plenty of choices. Naturally vegetarian signature pizzas include: Holy Shiitake, Veg Out, Keep It Greek, and Great White. The Build Your Own format accommodates any vegetable-only combination. Vegetarian appetizers include Pretzel Bites, Greek Feta Rollz, Cheese Bread, and Spinach Artichoke Dip. Several salads are also vegetarian-friendly.
For specific allergen information, speak directly with the staff at your location or check the official Mellow Mushroom website. Common allergens include gluten, dairy, eggs, tree nuts (in some sauces), and shellfish. This independent website cannot provide guaranteed allergen information — always confirm with the restaurant kitchen if you have a serious food allergy or dietary requirement.

Ordering & Locations

Order through the official Mellow Mushroom website (mellowmushroom.com) or app for pickup or delivery. You can also use third-party apps: DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub at most locations. Ordering directly through the official site is recommended because it shows exact current prices for your specific location and avoids third-party service fees (if choosing pickup).
Over 160 locations across the United States, with the heaviest concentration in the southeastern states (Georgia, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, Florida, Alabama). The brand has expanded nationally over the years. Use the location finder on mellowmushroom.com to find the nearest restaurant to you.
Mellow Mushroom is typically walk-in at most locations. However, for large groups (8 or more people), calling ahead is strongly recommended to ensure adequate seating. During major sports events, peak weekend evenings (Friday-Saturday 7–9pm), and holidays, wait times can be significant. Calling ahead to check wait times or secure a table for larger parties prevents frustrating waits.
Catering availability varies by location — some have dedicated catering programs with package pricing and event spaces; others handle catering through large pickup orders only. Contact your specific local Mellow Mushroom directly to discuss catering options for your event. The pizza format is naturally well-suited to feeding groups, and most locations can accommodate catering orders with advance notice.
Hours vary significantly by location. Most Mellow Mushroom restaurants open around 11am–12pm for lunch and close between 10pm–12am. Weekend hours may extend later; some locations close earlier on Sundays. Because each location is independently managed, hours can differ significantly from the general pattern. Always check the specific hours for your location on mellowmushroom.com or call directly.
